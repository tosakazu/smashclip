# Scene Tag Definitions

SmashClip uses 20 scene tags organized in three layers to describe the tactical context and presentation of each KO clip in Super Smash Bros. Ultimate.

## Technique Layer (5 tags)

Tags describing the mechanical execution of the KO.

| Tag | Definition | Example |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| Combo KO | A KO achieved through a multi-hit combo sequence (3+ connected hits leading to the finishing blow). | Pikachu chains nair into up-air into thunder for a kill at 90%. |
| Zero-to-death | A combo or sequence that takes the opponent from 0% damage all the way to a KO without dropping the advantage. | Luigi lands a grab at 0% and converts into a dair spike off stage for the stock. |
| Edgeguarding | Going offstage to intercept and KO the opponent during their recovery back to the stage. | Joker jumps offstage and hits the recovering opponent with a fair to send them past the blast zone. |
| One-turn kill | A KO from a single opening or interaction, not necessarily a combo -- e.g. a raw smash attack at high percent. | Ganondorf lands an uncharged forward smash on a shielding opponent at 120% for the kill. |
| Tech chase | Reacting to or predicting the opponent's tech option (neutral getup, roll, getup attack) after a knockdown. | After knocking the opponent down at ledge, the player reads the tech roll in and punishes with a charged smash. |

## Context Layer (13 tags)

Tags describing the situational context in which the KO occurs.

| Tag | Definition | Example |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| KO race | Both players are at KO percent and the outcome hinges on who lands the killing blow first. | Both players sit above 140%, and one lands a stray back-air for the stock. |
| Close-range | The KO sequence happens at point-blank range or in a close-quarters scramble. | Two players trade jabs at melee range until one lands a tilt that kills. |
| Ledge trapping | Pressuring the opponent's ledge options (neutral getup, roll, jump, getup attack) to score the KO. | The player covers ledge jump with an up-smash to close the stock. |
| Landing punish | Punishing the opponent's landing with an anti-air or juggle to score the KO. | The player catches the opponent landing with an up-air that kills off the top. |
| Neutral | The KO originates from a neutral game interaction where neither player has clear stage advantage. | Both players space aerials in neutral, and one converts a stray hit into a kill confirm. |
| Read | The KO results from a correct hard read on the opponent's option (e.g. predicting a roll, airdodge, or getup). | The player charges a smash attack at the spot where the opponent rolls, catching the roll endpoint. |
| Whiff punish | Punishing the opponent's missed attack (whiffed move) with a KO. | The opponent whiffs a forward smash, and the player punishes with a grab into back throw for the kill. |
| Approach | The killer initiates and closes space to land the KO, rather than waiting or reacting. | The player dashes in with a short-hop aerial to start the kill sequence. |
| Trade | Both players hit each other simultaneously, but the killer's attack results in the KO. | Both players swing at the same time, but one move has higher knockback and kills. |
| Trap | The killer places a lingering hitbox or projectile to control space, and it results in the KO. | Snake's C4 detonates on the opponent walking into the trap zone. |
| Mash | The killer escapes a disadvantage state (combo, pressure) by mashing out an attack that leads to a KO. | While being juggled, the player mashes nair and catches the opponent off guard for a kill. |
| Invincibility | The KO involves the use of invincibility frames (e.g. ledge getup invincibility, move super armor). | The player uses ledge getup invincibility to armor through an attack and punish with an up-B that kills. |
| Punish | The KO is a guaranteed punish on the opponent's unsafe move (e.g. shieldgrab after a blocked smash attack). | The player shields a forward smash and punishes with a grab into forward throw for the kill. |

## Meta Layer (7 tags)

Tags describing presentation and non-gameplay aspects.

| Tag | Definition | Example |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| Notable commentary | The commentators react to this KO with excitement, surprise, or analysis. | The commentator shouts and breaks down the sequence after a clutch stock take. |
| Funny moment | The KO involves a humorous or unexpected element. | A player accidentally self-destructs but it counts as a KO due to the blast zone interaction. |
| Coaching | A coach is heard giving advice or reacting during the clip. | A coach calls out "DI away!" from behind the player during the sequence. |
| Player cam | The player camera is shown during or immediately after the KO. | The broadcast cuts to the player's face as they react to closing out the set. |
| Misplay | The KO results from a clear mistake by the victim (e.g. misinput, failed recovery, dropped combo). | The opponent misinputs their recovery move and falls to the blast zone. |
| SD | Self-destruct: the victim loses the stock by going offstage themselves, not from a direct hit. | The opponent tries to edgeguard but overextends and fails to recover. |
| Time up | The match ends by time running out, not by a direct KO. | The timer hits zero and the player with the stock lead wins. |
